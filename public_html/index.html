Anton Zaytsev
Works About Resume

THESIS

Thesis is the year-long independent research project that acts as the capstone course for my Reed College education. Presently I am working under the guidance of Dr. Sameh Helmy to create a photoresponsive polymer that reversibly contracts upon exposure to light, with anticipated completion in May 2018.

SUNRISE SIMULATOR

Dawn, my DIY sunrise simulator, is a hacked consumer RGB LED strip operated by a Raspberry Pi server in my bedroom. Dawn simulates a sunrise during the hour leading up to waking time in order to awaken refreshed even on dark winter mornings.

POND

Pond is an interactive installation art project displayed every year at Reed College's Renn Fayre festival. The piece consists of a field of swaying jars that interactively change color as they move.

"MOBILE" TELEPHONES

The mobile phones are a current project to take a pair of old-school landline telephones and connect them via battery-powered ESP wifi modules to make them wireless. This project will become a public art piece that will enable participants to talk to one another across a distance through rogue old-fashioned telephones.

TETRIS ART HACK

During the Palo Alto Code:Art 2017 festival, I (consentually) hacked an installation by Mateo Garcia to play Tetris on his 30-foot tall hanging LED display.

INDUCTION HEATING

Building an induction heater was my first real introduction to electrical and hardware engineering. This project was a two-year quest that began with learning about rectifiers and advanced to designing and testing a 1.5 kW H-bridge circuit.

DIY PCB PRODUCTION

Before I began using the fast and affordable PCB printing facilities I use today, I developed a technique for DIY PCB lithographic etching using concentrated ferric chloride and Reed College's laser cutter.

LASER CUTTING

A collection of some of my prototyping, demonstrations, and tools built using the Reed College laser cutting lab.

ROBOTICS

In high school I was a member of FIRST Robotics Team 192 where I worked with a team to build hundred-pound robots for an annual competition on a six-week time frame. As head welder, design lead for one of the robot components, and machinist, my responsibilities taught me advanced mechanical design, implementation, and team management.
