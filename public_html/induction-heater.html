INDUCTION HEATING

Building an induction heater was my first real introduction to electrical and hardware engineering. This project was a two-year quest that began with learning about rectifiers and advanced to designing and testing a 1.5 kW H-bridge circuit. Induction heating is a highly advanced application of power electronics involving high voltages, high currents, and high frequencies that was far too difficult for a beginning hardware engineer to grapple. Nevertheless, employing the most careful safety measures, I worked through two years of trial and error to become the experienced hardware engineer I am today.

Early prototypes consisted of MOSFETs and capacitors soldered point-to-point in an attempt at building a half-bridge. Countless blown transistors and destroyed microcontrollers later, after researching circuit design and interviewing electrical engineer friends, the latest prototype is a professional-grade PCB with a functional high-power H-bridge and built-in analytical tools operated by an ARM-based microcontroller. This project has taken my skills very far, but has unfortunately been shelved due to budget problems and the fact that my task of heating metal for blacksmithing was solved with a homemade propane forge.
